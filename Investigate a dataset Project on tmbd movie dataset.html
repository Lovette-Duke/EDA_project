Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue.

The attributes in this dataset are id, imdb_id, popularity, budget, revenue, original_title, cast, homepage, director, tagline, keywords, overview, runtime, genres, production_companies, release_date, vote_count, vote_average, release_year, budget_adj, revenue_adj.

Certain columns, like ‘cast’ and ‘genres’, contain multiple values separated by pipe (|) characters. The final two columns ending with “_adj” show the budget and revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars, accounting for inflation over time.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

The question for this analysis is

What drives movie revenue?

Conclusions¶

The revenue from movies seem to be on a down word trend after the year 2000 inspite of the budget allocated or skyrocketing popularity, although budget does have some correlation to revenue, popularity also does have some correlation but not enough to be confident for higher revenue.

Limitations : While this dataset has the revenue, the actual profit from movies was not considered and I cannot tell from this analysis if the downward trend is bad for business. Also, there are millions of movies produced and released each year and this dataset only contains about 10,000 observations which is not sufficient to make conclutions on the current state of revenue generated in the movies industry.
